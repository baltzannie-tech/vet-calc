1 · TIVA Agent Guidance
Propofol (Propoflo Plus)
RVC: Limit to 2-3 hrs in CATS. Contains benzyl alcohol - lethal doses possible at sustained 24 mg/kg/hr. Heinz-body anaemia with repeat daily dosing. Drug + sets discard at 12 h.
Propofol-Lipuro Vet
No published max duration. Drug + infusion sets must be discarded after 12 hours per SPC. Preferred for longer or repeat cases.
Alfaxalone (Multidose)
1 h licensed; case reports/prospective studies support up to 3 h with adequate sedation premed. Drug + sets discard at 12 h. Less cardiovascular depression than propofol.
Ketamine (with propofol)
Cats: 2 mg/kg ket + 2 mg/kg prop bolus, then 10 mg/kg/hr at 1:1 admixture. Provides analgesia + anaesthesia in one syringe. Avoid in known seizure history.
2 · TIVA Admixture Compatibility
✓
Propofol + alfentanil - smooth induction without premed. Useful for boar/aggressive cases.
✓
Propofol + ketamine - 1:1 admixture, 10 mg/kg/hr in cats.
✓
Propofol + fentanyl (generic) - stable. Reduces propofol requirement.
✓
Alfaxalone + fentanyl - alfax dose reduction ~15% with fentanyl 10 mcg/kg/hr (Dhuisser 2017).
⚠
Propofol + REMIFENTANIL - DO NOT MIX. Rapidly forms precipitates. Run as separate infusions.
3 · Sodium Nitroprusside Prep
  1. Remove 310 ml from a 500 ml saline bag and discard (leaving 190 ml).
  2. Cover the bag completely with the provided tin-foil sleeve (photodegradation).
  3. Reconstitute SNP vial (50 mg) with 5 ml saline.
  4. Add 50 mg SNP to the 190 ml saline → final 250 mcg/ml.
  5. Draw up 50 ml for the syringe driver.
  6. Attach an anti-syphon extension set (mandatory - prevents bolus).
  7. Cover syringe with tin foil.
  8. Run through extension set to prime.
  9. Attach in syringe driver.
  10. Calculate rate on the CRI tab using Nitroprusside (Dog), conc 0.25 mg/ml.
  11. Recheck BP every 15 min initially. Target MAP 60-80 mmHg.

5 · Phosphate (Glycophos) - Dilution
Dilution 1: 1.6 ml Glycophos in 18.4 ml saline → 0.08 mmol/ml
Dilution 2: 4 ml Glycophos in 46 ml saline → 0.08 mmol/ml
Dilution 3: 20 ml Glycophos in 230 ml saline → 0.08 mmol/ml
⚠ Glycophos contains 1 mmol P + 2 mmol Na per ml. NEVER co-infuse with calcium-containing fluids (Hartmann's, Ca gluconate) - forms calcium phosphate precipitate. Compatible: 0.9% NaCl, 5% glucose, Plasma-Lyte 148, 20% mannitol.
